# Statistical Data Analysis Sprint 📊

This project focuses on bridging the gap between theoretical statistics and practical business applications. As a Junior Data Analyst, I explored two distinct domains—Public Health and the Automotive Industry—to derive actionable insights from complex datasets.

## 🎯 Project Overview
The mission was to master statistical concepts and apply them to real-world data to produce reliable, data-driven conclusions.

The project is divided into three phases:
1.  **Documentation:** Defining core statistical pillars.
2.  **Health Dataset:** Guided analysis of global health spending and life expectancy.
3.  **MPG Dataset:** Semi-autonomous analysis of automotive fuel efficiency and trends.

---

## 🧠 Phase 1: Statistical Foundations
Detailed documentation of the following concepts:
* **Descriptive Stats:** Mean, Median, Variance, Std Deviation, Histograms, and Boxplots.
* **Sampling:** Population vs. Sample, Sampling bias, and Estimation.
* **Distributions:** Normal, Bernoulli, Binomial, Poisson, Student's t, and Chi-square.
* **Inferential Stats:** Central Limit Theorem (CLT) and Hypothesis Testing ($H_0$ vs $H_1$, p-values).

---

## 🏥 Phase 2: Public Health Analysis
**Objective:** Analyze the relationship between healthcare spending and life expectancy.

### Key Tasks:
* **Data Cleaning:** Distinguishing between discrete and continuous variables.
* **Variable Analysis:** In-depth study of `Spending_USD` and `Life_Expectancy`.
* **Comparative Study:** Evolution of health spending in Germany vs. Great Britain.
* **Correlation:** Calculating Pearson correlation for the US (2000–2020).
* **Hypothesis Testing:** Testing a politician's claim regarding US life expectancy growth with a **98% confidence level ($\alpha = 0.02$)**.

---

## 🚗 Phase 3: Automotive Data (MPG)
**Objective:** Apply statistical logic to investigate vehicle performance and manufacturing trends.

### Key Tasks:
* **Performance Metrics:** Analyzing `mpg` (miles per gallon) and its true business meaning.
* **Trend Analysis:** How fuel consumption relates to `horsepower` and `model_year`.
* **Weight Evolution:** Tracking the average weight of American cars from 1970–1982.
* **Categorical Relationships:** Analyzing `cylinders` and `origin` to identify manufacturing shifts over time.

---

## 🛠️ Tools Used
* **Python:** Primary programming language.
* **Pandas:** Data manipulation and cleaning.
* **Matplotlib & Seaborn:** Statistical data visualization.
* **SciPy/Statsmodels:** Advanced statistical testing and correlation.

* Ability to identify and interpret **outliers** that skew business data.
* Rigorous application of **Hypothesis Testing** to justify conclusions.
* Visualizing complex relationships between variables to tell a clear data story.
